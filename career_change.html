How do you navigate a complete career change later in life? Is it even possible?

Apart from some early jobs working in retail, most of my working life was spent as an administrator - over 20 years in fact. At some point a manager noticed some potential in me, and I ultimately ended up as Data Manager for the company, heading up a small team. This was something I was good at and enjoyed. I was constantly challenged, and enjoyed learning new softwares and technologies; but when my first grandson was born I wanted a closer relationship, so moved away and had to start over.

Initially, I took a job I thought had some potential for growth. On the surface it had a lot going for it; it was a 10 minute drive away, the offices overlooked green fields, the people were friendly. Sadly, I quickly realised it might not be what I hoped. I stuck with it for 18 months until I got a lucky break when my son told me about a job that was available at his firm for an embedded software tester. He had, apparently, informed his managers that while I didn't have the skills, qualifications or experience required for the position, I could absolutely do the job. I was 61, and had no idea what an embedded software tester was.

Luckily, my son knows me well. I requested a face-to-face interview as I don't do well at telephone interviews (I babble...), which I was granted and somehow convinced them that I could do the job and although I had no experience I had plenty of transferable skills (this, by the way, is absolutely key)

The learning curve has been steep, including learning a programming language in my own time. My son's faith in me, and the company's trust in his judgement wasn't something I was prepared to squander. I've worked hard and learn something new at least once a day. Yes, with software tester skills I would have had a quicker start. Or even some industry knowledge (a working knowledge of how video works might have helped).

So, yes, it is possible to take a new path later in life. Age shouldn't define what you're capable of. I did have a leg up of course. Would I have got the job without my son? Honestly, I don't know. If I could have persuaded them to interview me, I think it's possible. But, yes, I got lucky.
